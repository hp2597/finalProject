Economics

Understanding how markets work and why economic principles matter for everyone.

Key Principles

  • Incentives Matter: People respond to incentives in predictable ways, often producing unintended consequences when incentives are ignored.
  • Trade-offs Are Universal: There are no solutions, only trade-offs. Every economic decision involves costs and benefits.
  • Knowledge Is Dispersed: No single person or committee can possess all the knowledge needed for effective central planning.
  • Prices Convey Information: Market prices communicate crucial information about scarcity, demand, and value across society.

Notable Insights

"The first lesson of economics is scarcity: there is never enough of anything to fully satisfy all those who want it. The first lesson of politics is to disregard the first lesson of economics."

"It is hard to imagine a more stupid or more dangerous way of making decisions than by putting those decisions in the hands of people who pay no price for being wrong."

Immigration

Analyzing migration flows, labor impacts, incentives, and policy outcomes.

Key Principles

  • Pushâ€“Pull Factors: People migrate in response to conditions at home (push) and opportunities abroad (pull).
  • Labor Market Effects: Immigration changes the supply of labor, affecting wages, employment, and economic growth over time.
  • Policy Incentives: Legal rules, enforcement, and benefits shape migrant decisions more than rhetoric does.
  • Assimilation & Institutions: Integration outcomes depend on culture, local institutions, and expectations on both sides.

  • Public Choice: Government actors are people with their own incentives and constraints, not benevolent abstractions.
